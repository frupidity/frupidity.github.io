Frupidity

Witty subtitle here

Airport

As you would expect from any city-slicking frugal person, I do not own a car. My bicycle is my main mode of transportation. The perks of getting around by bicycle are enormous. Not only do I get a built in 50 minutes of exercise 5 days a week on my commute to work, but I get to see the world at a slower pace. I’m often the person to tell friends about random places or events in town, thanks to my bicycling adventures. Bill Nye is even a big proponent of bicycle transportation!

That being said, there are definitely times when my lack of a car makes life a little more complicated…

Mr. Frupid and I travel to Florida to visit our families every December. Southern Florida is about as far as you can get from Seattle in the continental U.S., so the travel day is painful at the best of times. And this particular year was definitely not the best of times.

We (not surprisingly) opted to take the cheapest flight we could find, which left Seattle at 6:00 AM, without thinking through the logistics of getting to the airport. Our default means of getting from our house to the airport is to take a bus and then connect to the light rail, but unfortunately neither run between 1:00 and 5:00 AM. At this point, we were relatively new to Seattle and felt bad pushing our friend/acquaintance relationships into awkward territory by asking for a 3:30 AM ride to the airport. Another option was taking a taxi, but we weren’t interested in paying anywhere near the asking rate. So we settled on what seemed at the time to be the most rational solution - go to bed by 8:00 PM so we could wake up at midnight, walk half a mile in the dark with our rolly suitcases to a less convenient bus, connect to the last light rail of the night downtown, and then hang out at the airport for an extra 4 hours before our flight. What could go wrong?!

Well, for starters, waking up to an alarm about an hour after actually falling asleep feels similar to how I assume attempting to blow dry your hair under water feels, painful and counterproductive. We somehow survived the initial shock though, and got to the bus stop just in time. I was feeling like a master planner at this point, though the feeling was short lived. When we got to the light rail station it was deserted. As we attempted to walk down the still escalator, a janitor came out of nowhere and yelled at us for being in the station, informing us that the last train departed 30 minutes earlier. I couldn’t decide whether I wanted to punch the guy or cry, so I just yelled “but that’s not what Google told us!” as if he would understand that Google is some all-knowing, all powerful God who would defy the schedule and make that train appear, damnit! (Sidenote, I’m not actually sure this is an inaccurate depiction of Google, we just may need to wait another few years for that to come to fruition.)

Fortunately, our bloodshot eyes, slow cognition, and nonsensical outburt weren’t quite enough to convince the janitor that we were just high out of our minds, so he felt pity on us and told us that another bus that stopped right outside the station would almost get us to the airport…almost.

If you’ve ever been skeptical of people who take the bus at 1:00 AM, stay skeptical. Especially if that bus goes straight through the shadier neighborhoods in town. For some reason this bus was packed, so Mr. Frupid and I had to split up. I’m pretty sure multiple drug deals went on in the seats around me. Needless to say, I was thoroughly uncomfortable on this journey. But we survived, and about an hour later we were at the final stop, almost to the airport.

We were actually only about a mile away from the airport at this point. Unfortunately, it was a mile of large highway with plenty of run-down hotels advertising “hourly rentals” and the occasional lady of the night lingering out front. But alas, we walked our trusty rolly suitcases through sketch-ville. We couldn’t help but giggle at the thought of walking onto airport property - we doubted that anyone had tried it before. But we were going to do it, and at 2:00 AM to boot! About halfway to the airport, we spotted the car rental depot and noticed the office was open. We decided to go in and ask for a lift to the departures area.

There were two employees in the office. The woman working clearly wasn’t pleased to have her middle of the night lull interrupted and wanted nothing to do with us. Fortunately, though, the man working felt pity on us and agreed to drive us to the departures area. So at about 2:30 AM, more than 2 hours after our 20 minute drive of an adventure started, we arrived in style via the car rental shuttle. After that, the experience was pretty smooth sailing. We unsuccessfully attempted to nap in airport chairs with our sunglasses on for 2 hours then started the “normal” travel experience.

I will concede that it absolutely would have made more sense to just take the red-eye or chalk up the $50 for a cab. On the other hand, we were still young and a night of missed sleep (and a few sketchy encounters) didn’t kill us. One thing is for sure, we’ll never forget that night!

January 2, 2017
